Alan Turing

The man who laid foundation of modern computing

Here's the timeline of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912: Alan Mathison Turing is born in Maida Vale, London, England.

  • 1928–1931: Turing studies at Sherborne School, displaying exceptional aptitude in mathematics.

  • 1931–1934: Turing continues his education at King's College, Cambridge, where he studies mathematics.

  • 1936: Turing publishes his seminal paper "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of the Turing machine, a theoretical model of computation.

  • 1938–1939: Turing works at the Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park, where he contributes to breaking the German Enigma code during World War II.

  • 1945: Turing is awarded the Order of the British Empire (OBE) for his wartime services.

  • 1946: Turing designs the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE), an early concept for a stored-program computer.

  • 1950: Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the Turing Test as a measure of artificial intelligence.

  • 1952: Turing is arrested and charged with "gross indecency" for his homosexuality, which was criminalized in the UK at the time.

  • 1952: Turing undergoes chemical castration as an alternative to imprisonment.

  • 1954: Alan Turing dies by cyanide poisoning. The official cause of death is suicide, though there has been ongoing speculation and debate.

  • 1966: The term "Turing machine" is officially coined in honor of Alan Turing's contributions to theoretical computer science.

  • 2013: Turing is granted a posthumous royal pardon for his conviction for homosexuality.

  • 2019: The Bank of England announces that Alan Turing will be featured on the new £50 banknote.
